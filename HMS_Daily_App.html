Interoception Training
Rewiring how your brain reads body signals
▼
✓
Morning Body Scan Morning
The ADAPT trial's core intervention. Lie or sit, slowly move attention from feet to head. Name each sensation neutrally — "warmth," "tension" — without judging it. If your mind says "something's wrong," note it as a thought, not a fact.
Duration5–10 minutes daily on waking
✓
Neutral Sensation Labelling Anytime
When you notice a body sensation (racing heart, gut twist, dizziness), pause and name it in plain language: "My heart is beating faster." Then ask: is there a real threat, or is my nervous system misfiring?
✓
Heartbeat Awareness Daily
Research shows hypermobile people have heightened cardiac interoception that amplifies anxiety. Gently attending to the heartbeat in a calm state trains the brain to feel it without fear.
How to do itSit quietly. Hand on chest or wrist. 2 minutes simply noticing your heartbeat — not counting, just acknowledging. Build to 5 minutes.
⚡
Autonomic Regulation
Vagal toning — directly addresses dysautonomia-anxiety link
▼
✓
4-6-8 Breathing Twice daily
The extended exhale directly activates the parasympathetic nervous system via the vagus nerve. Mechanistically appropriate for HMS, not just general relaxation.
PatternInhale 4 counts → Hold 6 → Exhale slowly 8. The long exhale is the active ingredient. 6–10 cycles, morning and before bed.
✓
Humming / Vocalisation Anytime
The vagus nerve runs through the vocal cords. Low-frequency humming activates vagal tone directly. 5–10 minutes. Singing and gargling work too.
✓
Orienting Practice On waking
Signals "safety" to the amygdala, structurally altered in hypermobile individuals. Let your eyes slowly move around the room, landing on objects. Soft gaze. Slow neck turns. Notice sounds. Take a slow breath. 2 minutes.
✓
Cold Water Face Immersion Acute anxiety
Triggers the mammalian dive reflex — one of the fastest ways to reduce acute autonomic arousal. Used in DBT for HMS patients.
How to do itHold breath, submerge face in cold water 15–30 sec. Or cold cloth on forehead/cheeks/eyes. Heart rate drops within seconds via vagus nerve.
⚠ Not if you have heart conditions or active PoTS symptoms

Cognitive Tools (CBT)
Most studied psychological intervention for HMS
▼
✓
Daily Sensation Journal 5 min
Core ADAPT trial homework. Distinguishes body signals from emotional interpretations — the key cognitive skill in HMS anxiety.
Daily prompts1. What body sensation caused worry today?
2. What did my mind say it meant?
3. A neutral, factual description?
4. Did it pass on its own?
✓
ACT: Values-Based Action Weekly
ACT reduces pain catastrophisation and depression in HMS. Focus on doing what matters despite symptoms — not fighting them.
Weekly promptWhat did I avoid this week because of pain or anxiety? What is one small step toward something I value this week, even imperfectly?

Sleep (HMS-Specific)
Poor sleep amplifies pain sensitivity and anxiety
▼
✓
Consistent Wake Time Non-negotiable
Autonomic dysfunction worsens with circadian disruption. Same wake time daily (±30 min, even weekends) anchors your rhythm and regulates cortisol, heart rate variability, and gut motility simultaneously.
✓
Joint Support Positioning Before bed
Night-time micro-subluxations disrupt sleep and feed next-day anxiety.
PositionsSide: pillow between knees · Back: pillow under knees · Avoid stomach sleeping · Don't tuck hypermobile wrists/hands under head
✓
30-min Wind-Down Evening
The sensitised HMS nervous system needs longer to shift from sympathetic to parasympathetic. Dim lights → gentle muscle stretching (no end-range) → 4-6-8 breathing → body scan. No news or social media after.

Eating Habits
How and when matters as much as what
▼
✓
Small, Frequent Meals Every meal
Large meals increase pressure in a gut with lax connective tissue, worsening bloating and reflux. 4–5 smaller meals rather than 2–3 large ones. No meal should leave you very full.
✓
Upright 30 min After Eating Post-meal
Lax connective tissue around the lower oesophageal sphincter increases reflux risk. Gravity assists. Remain sitting or standing for 30–45 minutes after meals.
✓
Slow, Mindful Eating Every meal
Eating while anxious keeps the gut in sympathetic mode, slowing motility. 3 slow breaths before eating. Chew thoroughly. Put down utensils between bites. No screens.
✓
Hydration: 2–2.5L Daily All day
Dehydration worsens constipation and PoTS symptoms which affect gut motility. Sip steadily — don't drink large volumes at once. Small pinch of salt in water if you have orthostatic symptoms.

Low-FODMAP Quick Reference
Most evidence-backed dietary approach for HMS gut symptoms
▼

Limit / Test First

  • Garlic & onions
  • Wheat, rye, barley
  • Apples, pears, stone fruits
  • Cauliflower, mushrooms
  • Cow's milk, soft cheeses
  • Beans, lentils, chickpeas
  • Honey, HFCS
  • Cashews, pistachios
  • Artichokes, asparagus

Generally Tolerated

  • Rice, oats, quinoa, potatoes
  • Carrots, courgette, spinach
  • Blueberries, strawberries, kiwi
  • Bananas (unripe), oranges
  • Lactose-free dairy, hard cheeses
  • Eggs, chicken, fish, tofu
  • Almonds (max ~10), walnuts
  • Green beans, cucumber, tomatoes
⚠ Phase 1 elimination max 6 weeks. Use the Monash University FODMAP app for the most accurate food lists. Work with a dietitian — long-term strict FODMAP harms gut microbiome diversity.

Supplements & Nutrition
Based on documented deficiencies in HMS populations
▼
✓
Soluble Fibre Daily Diet
Regulates both diarrhea and constipation — relevant for the bidirectional motility issues in HMS. Psyllium husk (most studied for IBS), oats, soaked chia seeds. Start small, increase slowly, take with plenty of water.
✓
Probiotic Daily
Meta-analyses support probiotics for IBS. Lactobacillus rhamnosus GG and Bifidobacterium lactis have evidence for reducing diarrhea. Also supports the gut-brain axis and may modulate visceral hypersensitivity in HMS.
✓
Get Tested: Vit D, B12, Magnesium Ask GP
Significant deficiencies documented in HMS patients. Magnesium deficiency links to muscle cramping, gut spasm, anxiety, and poor sleep. Request full blood count, ferritin, serum B12, Vit D (25-OH), magnesium from your GP.
⚠ Don't self-supplement high doses before testing

Gut-Brain Axis
Stress management IS gut management in HMS
▼
✓
Post-Meal Parasympathetic Reset After meals
Digestion requires parasympathetic dominance. 2 minutes after meals: remain seated, 5 slow breaths (exhale longer than inhale), one hand on belly. This is active autonomic shifting, not just mindfulness.
✓
Food & Symptom Diary Daily 3 min
Standard in HMS gastroenterology clinics. Log: time → what you ate → symptoms within 2 hours → stress level (1–5) → stool type (Bristol Stool Chart). After 2–3 weeks, patterns become clear. Bring to your gastroenterologist.
✓
Post-Meal Walk (10–15 min) After meals
Accelerates gut transit time. Gentle walking after the main meal combines joint-safe aerobic activity with motility stimulation, and activates the vagal brake via baroreceptors in the legs.

Sitting / watching TV
Foot doming · Leg extension · Toe spreading
▼
Foot doming — press ball of foot into floor, toes long (not curled). Hold 5 sec. Activates intrinsic foot muscles critical for arch support.

Seated leg extension hold — extend one leg to near-straight (knee NOT locked), hold 5 sec, lower. Trains VMO (inner quad = key knee stabiliser in HMS).

Toe spreading — spread toes as wide as possible, hold 5 sec, release. Barefoot only.
Wk 1–4
Foot doming + toe spreading daily
Wk 5+
Add leg extension hold, increase to 10 sec

Commuting / Queuing
Soft-knee check · Rocking · Transport balance
▼
Soft-knee check — whenever standing still, check: are my knees locked? Unlock them. Do this dozens of times daily — it retrains your default joint position.

Heel-to-toe rocking — rock forward onto balls of feet, back to heels, in control. Unobtrusive enough to do anywhere.

Single-leg on transport — hold rail lightly (don't grip), lift one foot slightly. Moving vehicles create unpredictable perturbations — this is essentially a free wobble board.
Wk 1–4
Soft-knee habit + heel-to-toe rocking
Wk 5+
Single-leg on transport, increasing duration

Master progression timeline
Month 1
Bilateral stability, soft-knee habit, eyes open only. Building the habit stack. Check "are my knees locked?" constantly.
Month 2
Single-leg work, eyes open, near support. Somatosensory system, hip stabilisers, VMO activation.
Month 3
Eyes closed, head turns, short intervals. Vestibular integration — eliminating visual compensation.
Month 4+
Unstable surface (folded towel), full challenge. Reactive stability and running gait preparation. Compounding adaptation.
